§Theory

Under Neyman-orthogonality + cross-fitting, the first-order interaction between causal parameter θ and nuisance η vanishes:

D_η D_θ E[loss] = 0

This ensures that nuisance estimation error r_n doesn’t inflate the bias in θ̂. Instead, convergence is O_P(√n r_n²) rather than O_P(√n r_n).

Cross-fitting is the mechanism that enforces this: validation losses are i.i.d. conditional on training samples, preventing empirical process bias.
